Key Conversation Tips

üëÇ

Listen More Than You Talk

Your job is to understand their situation, not pitch services. Ask the question, then let them talk. Take notes. The more they share, the better positioned you are to help.

üéØ

Focus on Their Problem

Clients don't care about our 280+ projects - they care about their project. Frame everything in terms of how it helps them succeed, avoid delays, or reduce risk.

üìß

Always Follow Up

After any conversation where you learn about a potential opportunity, send a brief follow-up email within 24 hours. Reference what you discussed and offer a next step.

üìã

Report Back to Chris

If you uncover a real opportunity, let Chris know immediately. Include: client name, project details, their timeline, who they're talking to, and what they need.

Q1: "What's your timeline for getting this project connected to the grid?"

Why Ask: Timeline questions reveal urgency and development cycle stage.

Follow-Ups:

  • "Have you submitted your connection enquiry to the NSP yet?"
  • "What stage are you at - R0, R1, or somewhere in between?"
  • "Is there a financial close date driving that timeline?"
Tight timeline: "You'll want to get R1 studies started soon to avoid delays."
üò∞

Q2: "What's your biggest concern about the grid connection process?"

Why Ask: Invites clients to share pain points. Most have been burned before.

Follow-Ups:

  • "Have you had issues with connection studies on previous projects?"
  • "Is system strength a worry at your connection point?"
  • "What's the NSP been like to work with so far?"
Weak grid worries: "Weak grids are our specialty - we've successfully connected many projects in low SCR areas."
‚öôÔ∏è

Q3: "Have you selected your equipment supplier yet?"

Why Ask: Equipment selection directly impacts grid connection success.

Follow-Ups:

  • "Which OEMs are you considering?"
  • "Has the OEM provided validated PSS/E or PowerFactory models yet?"
  • "Are you looking at hybrid configurations - wind plus BESS?"
Comparing OEMs: "We can help evaluate them from a grid compliance perspective - some have better track records with AEMO."
üë•

Q4: "Who's handling your grid connection studies currently?"

Why Ask: Tells you if there's an incumbent, in-house capability, or opportunity.

Follow-Ups:

  • "How's that going so far?"
  • "Do you have in-house capability, or looking for external support?"
  • "Would you value a second opinion on any of the technical work?"
No one engaged: "Perfect timing - let me tell you about our approach and how we can help."
