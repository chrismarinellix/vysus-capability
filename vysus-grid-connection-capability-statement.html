Overcoming Your Grid Connection Challenges

How our specialized experts solve the industry's toughest problems

CLIENT CHALLENGE: IBR Stability & Control System Interactions EXTREME
The Problem: Inverter control systems interact with network impedance causing oscillations and voltage instability that can delay commissioning for months.

Our Solution: Deep understanding of converter controls, PLL dynamics, and EMT-level modeling to predict and prevent instabilities before commissioning.
Your Experts:
Dr. Anthony Morton - 20+ years power systems, IBR specialist Naveen Rajagopal - PSCAD/EMT expert, IBR modeling Dr. Ajith Tennakoon - Protection & control, 30+ years
CLIENT CHALLENGE: Sub-Synchronous Resonance & Equipment Damage Risk EXTREME
The Problem: Series compensation can excite mechanical shaft oscillations causing catastrophic turbine damage and voiding warranties.

Our Solution: Specialized EMT modeling and mechanical system analysis to identify risks early and design mitigation strategies that preserve warranties.
Your Experts:
Dr. Anthony Morton - SSR analysis specialist Naveen Rajagopal - PSCAD/EMTP-RV expert, TI studies Dr. Faraz Khan - Wind turbine mechanical systems
CLIENT CHALLENGE: Weak Grid Connections & Voltage Stability VERY HIGH
High X/R ratios and low short circuit ratios (SCR < 3) create voltage stability challenges, reactive power oscillations, and protection coordination issues. Requires sophisticated VRCS design, dynamic reactive compensation, and detailed stability analysis.
Our Experts:
Chris Marinelli - VRCS design, weak grid specialist Eduardo Jr Cruz Laygo - TSO experience, voltage control Ir. Amani Razif - Reactive power optimization
Protection Coordination in Changing Fault Levels VERY HIGH
IBR fault current contribution varies with operating conditions, creating protection blind spots. Traditional overcurrent protection fails with low fault currents from inverters. Requires advanced protection schemes, adaptive settings, and comprehensive coordination studies.
Our Experts:
Dr. Ajith Tennakoon - Former utility protection engineer Eduardo Jr Cruz Laygo - Network protection specialist Dr. Faraz Khan - Protection & control systems
Dynamic Model Development & AEMO Validation (200+ Simulations) HIGH
AEMO's Dynamic Model Acceptance Test Guideline requires 200+ simulations covering all operating conditions, fault types, and control modes. Models must match manufacturer data and pass all acceptance criteria. Time-consuming and technically demanding process requiring expert knowledge of PSS/E and PowerFactory.
Our Experts:
Dr. Anthony Morton - AEMO model validation expert Naveen Rajagopal - PSS/E & PowerFactory specialist Ir. Amani Razif - Model development & validation
Harmonic Resonance & Power Quality in Weak Grids HIGH
Inverter switching harmonics interact with network resonances creating voltage distortion exceeding IEC 61000 limits. Filter design must avoid creating new resonances while mitigating existing harmonics. Requires detailed frequency scan analysis and filter optimization.
